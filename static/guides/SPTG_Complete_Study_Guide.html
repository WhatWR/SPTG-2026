⚙️ 4 Forces of Flight 🔴 Must Know

  • LIFT — Generated by wings (Bernoulli's principle); acts upward perpendicular to flight path
  • WEIGHT — Gravity acting downward through center of gravity
  • THRUST — Engine propulsion; acts forward along flight path
  • DRAG — Aerodynamic resistance; acts opposite to direction of flight
✅ In straight & level flight:
Lift = Weight, Thrust = Drag

💨 Hypoxia 🔴 Must Know

🔴 Hypoxia is the #1 most tested aeromedical topic in aviation exams worldwide!

Definition & Types

TypeCauseExample
HypoxicLow O₂ partial pressureHigh altitude, pressurization failure
Hypemic (Anemic)Reduced O₂ carrying capacity of bloodCarbon monoxide poisoning, anemia
StagnantReduced blood flowHeart failure, pulling Gs, shock
HistotoxicCells can't use O₂Alcohol, drugs, cyanide poisoning

Time of Useful Consciousness (TUC)

AltitudeTUC
18,000 ft (5,500 m)20–30 minutes
25,000 ft (7,600 m)3–5 minutes
30,000 ft (9,100 m)1–2 minutes
40,000 ft (12,200 m)15–20 seconds
43,000 ft+ (13,000 m+)9–12 seconds

Symptoms (remember: DEAD)

  • D — Dizziness, decreased vision (blue/grey color first to go)
  • E — Euphoria, Errors in judgement
  • A — Apprehension, Air hunger (late sign)
  • D — Drowsiness, Decreased motor skills
⚠️ Hypoxia is insidious — pilot feels fine (euphoria) while performance degrades!

🔄 Spatial Disorientation 🔴 Must Know

The Leans

  • Most common SD illusion
  • Body feels banked while instruments show wings-level
  • Caused by undetected slow roll, then correction
  • Trust instruments — not body senses!

Graveyard Spiral

  • Sustained coordinated turn feels like straight and level
  • Pilot pulls back, tightening spiral
  • Usually fatal if not corrected with instruments

Coriolis Illusion

  • Head movement during turn causes tumbling sensation
  • Avoid head movements in IMC turns

Somatogravic Illusion

  • Rapid acceleration feels like nose-up pitch
  • Pilot pushes nose down — dangerous at low altitude
  • Common during catapult launch or go-around

Black-Hole Approach

  • Night approach over water/featureless terrain
  • No visual references — dangerously low approach

🫁 Decompression Sickness (DCS) 🟠 Likely

Causes

  • Rapid decompression → N₂ bubbles form in blood/tissues
  • Like opening a soda can — gas comes out of solution
  • Risk above 25,000 ft (8,000 m)

Types

  • Type I (Mild): Joint pain (the "bends"), skin itch
  • Type II (Serious): Respiratory (chokes), neurological (staggers), circulatory collapse

Treatment

  • 100% oxygen immediately
  • Descend to lowest safe altitude
  • Hyperbaric chamber treatment

Prevention

  • Pre-breathe 100% O₂ before ascent to high altitude
  • Wait 24 hours after SCUBA diving before flying
  • Cabin pressurized to equivalent of 6,000–8,000 ft

👁 Vision in Aviation 🟠 Likely

Night Vision

  • Rods (peripheral) — for low light, no color
  • Cones (central/fovea) — color vision, bright light
  • Dark adaptation: ~30–45 min (red light preserves)
  • Off-center viewing: look 10° away from target at night

Visual Illusions

  • Narrow runway → pilot thinks high → lands short
  • Wide runway → pilot thinks low → lands long
  • Upsloping runway → pilot thinks high → lands short
  • Downsloping runway → pilot thinks low → overshoot
  • Rain on windscreen → runway appears further

💊 Other Aeromedical Topics 🔵 Good to Know

Hyperventilation

  • Over-breathing → low CO₂ → symptoms mimic hypoxia
  • Tingling hands/feet, muscle spasms, light-headedness
  • Treatment: slow breathing, breathe into bag

Ear/Sinus Blocks

  • Eustachian tube equalizes middle ear pressure
  • Block occurs during descent with upper respiratory infection
  • Valsalva maneuver: pinch nose, blow gently

G-Forces

  • +Gz (head-to-seat): greyout → blackout → G-LOC
  • AGSM: Anti-G Straining Maneuver (tensing muscles)
  • G-tolerance: typically +4 to +6 Gz untrained
  • -Gz (negative G): redout

⚡ Physics Essentials 🟠 Likely

Newton's Laws

  • 1st (Inertia): Object stays at rest or motion unless acted on by force
  • 2nd (F=ma): Force = mass × acceleration
  • 3rd (Action-Reaction): Every action has equal & opposite reaction (basis of jet propulsion)

Bernoulli's Principle

  • Faster airflow = lower pressure
  • Wing curved on top → faster airflow → lower pressure above → LIFT
  • Also explains Venturi tube, carburetors

Thermodynamics

  • Standard atmosphere: -2°C per 1,000 ft (ISA lapse rate)
  • Boiling point of water: 100°C at sea level; lower at altitude
  • Absolute zero: -273.15°C = 0 Kelvin

Electromagnetic Spectrum (shortest to longest wavelength)

  • Gamma rays → X-rays → UV → Visible → IR → Microwave → Radio
  • Visible light: 400–700 nm (violet to red)
