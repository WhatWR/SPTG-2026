🌕 Earth's Moon — Key Facts

  • Distance from Earth: ~384,400 km (average)
  • Orbital period: 27.3 days (sidereal)
  • Same face always toward Earth (synchronous rotation)
  • No atmosphere, no liquid water on surface
  • Formed ~4.5 billion years ago (giant impact hypothesis)
  • Humans visited: 1969–1972 (Apollo 11–17)
  • Last human on Moon: Gene Cernan, Apollo 17, Dec 1972

🛸 ISS — International Space Station

  • Orbits at ~400 km altitude
  • Travels at ~27,600 km/h
  • Orbits Earth every ~90 minutes
  • Continuously occupied since November 2000
  • Built by: USA, Russia, ESA, Japan, Canada
  • Length of football field (~109m)
  • Retirement planned: 2030 (deorbit into Pacific Ocean)

🔭 Space Telescopes

  • Hubble — launched 1990, orbits at 547 km, visible + UV light
  • James Webb (JWST) — launched Dec 2021, infrared, at Lagrange Point L2 (~1.5M km from Earth) — sees earliest galaxies
  • Chandra — X-ray telescope
  • Spitzer — infrared (retired 2020)

🚀 Current & Notable Rockets

  • SLS (Space Launch System) — NASA's Artemis rocket
  • Falcon 9 / Falcon Heavy — SpaceX, reusable
  • Starship — SpaceX, largest ever, fully reusable (tested 2023–2024)
  • Ariane 6 — ESA (Europe)
  • Long March 5 — China (CNSA)

Which correctly ranks particles SMALLEST to LARGEST? 🔴 CONFIRMED in Mock B
Virus → Bacteria → PM2.5 → Pollen
▼
A) PM2.5 → Virus → Bacteria → Pollen
B) Virus → Bacteria → PM2.5 → Pollen
C) Bacteria → Virus → Pollen → PM2.5
D) Virus → PM2.5 → Bacteria → Pollen
Answer: B — Virus → Bacteria → PM2.5 → Pollen

Sizes (approximate):
• Virus: 0.02–0.3 µm (nanometers range)
• Bacteria: 0.5–5 µm
• PM2.5: up to 2.5 µm (fine particulate matter)
• Pollen: 10–100 µm (largest)

⚠️ Trap: PM2.5 means particles smaller than 2.5 µm, which is actually between bacteria and pollen in size
Correct order: Virus < Bacteria ≈ PM2.5 < Pollen
S2
At what dB level does sound reach the "Threshold of Pain"? 🔴 CONFIRMED in Mock B
130 dB
▼
A) 85 dB
B) 100 dB
C) 130 dB
D) 160 dB
Answer: C — 130 dB

Sound intensity reference points:
• 0 dB — Threshold of hearing
• 60 dB — Normal conversation
• 85 dB — Threshold of hearing damage (prolonged exposure)
• 120 dB — Jet engine at 100m (discomfort)
• 130 dB — Threshold of Pain
• 194 dB — Theoretical maximum in air

✅ Remember: 130 dB = Pain threshold
S3
Most accurate statement about blue light from screens and eye health? 🔴 CONFIRMED in Mock B
No permanent damage, but causes eye strain & sleep disruption
▼
A) Causes irreversible photochemical damage to the macula
B) Emits same radiation as direct sunlight
C) No scientific evidence of permanent physical eye disease; contributes to digital eye strain and sleep disruption
D) Primary cause of cataracts in young adults
Answer: C

Current scientific consensus:
• Blue light from screens is not strong enough to cause retinal damage (unlike UV from the sun)
• It does suppress melatonin production → disrupts sleep patterns
• It does contribute to digital eye strain (dryness, focusing fatigue)
• It does NOT cause cataracts, blindness, or permanent eye disease at screen levels

✅ Key: No permanent damage, but real effects on sleep + eye comfort
S4
Which wind condition is most beneficial for a pilot during takeoff and landing? 🔴 CONFIRMED in Mock B
Headwind both times
▼
A) Headwind during takeoff AND headwind during landing
B) Headwind during takeoff and tailwind during landing
C) Tailwind during takeoff and headwind during landing
D) Tailwind during takeoff and tailwind during landing
Answer: A — Headwind both during takeoff AND landing

Headwind = wind blowing toward the aircraft (from the front)

Why headwind is better:
• Increases airspeed relative to the aircraft without increasing ground speed
• Aircraft reaches takeoff airspeed at lower ground speed → shorter runway needed
• During landing: aircraft can slow down faster and land in shorter distance

⚠️ Tailwind increases ground speed — requires longer runway — dangerous!
That's why runways are chosen into the wind direction.
S5
Lightning flash seen, thunder heard 4 seconds later. Speed of sound = 340 m/s. How far away? 🟠 CONFIRMED in Mock B Q72
1,360 m
▼
A) 680 m
B) 1,020 m
C) 1,360 m
D) 1,700 m
Answer: C — 1,360 m

Light travels essentially instantaneously (speed of light ≈ ∞ for practical purposes)
Sound travels at 340 m/s

Distance = speed × time = 340 × 4 = 1,360 m = 1.36 km

✅ Quick rule: Every 3 seconds = ~1 km from lightning
S6
Radio station at 900 MHz. Speed of light = 3×10⁸ m/s. What is the wavelength? 🟠 CONFIRMED in Mock B Q68
0.33 m
▼
A) 0.033 m
B) 0.33 m
C) 3.3 m
D) 33 m
Answer: B — 0.33 m

Wave equation: λ = c / f
λ = 3×10⁸ / 900×10⁶ = 3×10⁸ / 9×10⁸ = 1/3 ≈ 0.33 m

• λ = wavelength (meters)
• c = speed of light (3×10⁸ m/s)
• f = frequency (Hz) — 900 MHz = 900 × 10⁶ Hz

S8
Which force makes an aircraft turn? 🔴 CONFIRMED in Mock B Q13
Horizontal component of lift
▼
A) Centrifugal force
B) Horizontal component of lift
C) Rudder force
D) Excessive Thrust
Answer: B — Horizontal component of lift

When an aircraft banks (rolls), the lift vector tilts:
• Vertical component of lift → supports the aircraft's weight
• Horizontal component of lift → provides centripetal force → turns the aircraft

The rudder coordinates the turn (prevents slipping/skidding) but does NOT primarily cause the turn. The ailerons bank the wings, and the banked lift vector provides the turning force.

✅ Key: Bank angle → lift tilts → horizontal component → turn
⚡ Physics — Key Concepts & Formulas
🎯

Mock Exam B has ~30 physics questions. These are the most common types that appear: kinematics (v = u + at, s = ut + ½at²), Newton's laws (F = ma), energy/work/power (P = W/t), wave equations (λ = c/f), and simple circuits (V = IR).

📝 Worked Examples (from your Mock Exam B)
P1
Airplane touches down at 70 m/s, decelerates at 3 m/s². Speed after 10 seconds? Mock B Q3
40 m/s
▼
A) 30 m/s
B) 40 m/s
C) 50 m/s
D) 60 m/s
Answer: B — 40 m/s

Use: v = u + at
v = 70 + (−3)(10) = 70 − 30 = 40 m/s

Deceleration = negative acceleration, so a = −3 m/s²
P2
Object dropped from 80 m. How long to reach ground? (g = 10 m/s²) Mock B Q15
4 s
▼
A) 2 s
B) 3 s
C) 4 s
D) 5 s
Answer: C — 4 s

Use: s = ut + ½at², where u = 0 (dropped from rest)
80 = 0 + ½(10)t² = 5t²
t² = 16 → t = 4 s
P3
Inelastic collision: 2,000 kg car at 30 m/s hits stationary 3,000 kg truck. Common velocity? Mock B Q46
12 m/s
▼
A) 8 m/s
B) 10 m/s
C) 12 m/s
D) 15 m/s
Answer: C — 12 m/s

Conservation of momentum: m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ = (m₁+m₂)v
(2000)(30) + (3000)(0) = (2000+3000)v
60,000 = 5,000v
v = 12 m/s
P4
Light bulb: 24 Ω resistance, 12 V battery. Current and power? Mock B Q93
I = 0.5 A, P = 6 W
▼
A) I = 0.5 A, P = 6 W
B) I = 1.0 A, P = 12 W
C) I = 2.0 A, P = 48 W
D) I = 0.5 A, P = 3 W
Answer: A — I = 0.5 A, P = 6 W

I = V/R = 12/24 = 0.5 A
P = VI = 12 × 0.5 = 6 W
Or: P = V²/R = 144/24 = 6 W ✅
